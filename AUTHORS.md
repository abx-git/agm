The Architecture Graph Method (AGM) grew out of practical work with AI coding agents and architecture documentation. I like to try smart approaches early — even when they are not finished yet — as long as they show real potential. This repository is one of those experiments: feedback, case studies, and pull requests are welcome.

## Field experience

The method has been applied to **very large, elemental core systems** in an enterprise context — the kind of applications that sit at the center of a company's IT landscape. In practice it worked reliably: deterministic traversal, stable interface contracts, and usable Architecture Work on top of the graph.

The critical success factor was not a perfect Bootstrap, but **continuous Refinement**. Large systems never reach “documentation done”; the Blueprint stays alive through regular, targeted deepening sessions whenever domains, integrations, or quality attributes evolve. Treat Refinement as an ongoing architectural practice, not an optional follow-up.
